# Corporate Criminal Risk Prevention in Shenzhen: A 2026 Compliance Checklist

*Last updated: January 2026 | Reading time: 15 minutes*

Shenzhen, as China's innovation hub and home to thousands of technology companies and financial institutions, presents unique corporate criminal risks. This comprehensive checklist helps businesses identify and mitigate these risks before they become legal crises.

## The Corporate Criminal Risk Landscape

Corporate crimes (单位犯罪) in China are governed by **Articles 30-31 of the PRC Criminal Law** (《刑法》第三十至三十一条). Unlike individual crimes, corporate crimes involve the organization as a whole, with both the entity and its responsible personnel facing penalties.

### 2. What is the "dual punishment" system for corporate crimes?

Under Article 31 of the Criminal Law (《刑法》第三十一条):

> "For a unit that commits a crime, the unit shall be fined, and the persons who are directly in charge and the other persons who are directly responsible for the crime shall be punished."

This means:
- **The company** faces criminal fines (criminal law fine, 罚金)
- **The legal representative** and responsible managers face imprisonment
- **Individual employees** who directly participated face personal criminal liability

### 3. What should be included in a corporate compliance program?

A comprehensive corporate compliance program should cover:

**Governance Structure**:
- Written compliance policies approved by the board
- Designated Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)
- Compliance committee with cross-departmental representation
- Regular board-level compliance reporting

**Risk Assessment**:
- Annual identification of criminal risk areas specific to your industry
- Mapping of business processes against Criminal Law provisions
- Assessment of third-party relationships and supply chain risks
- Evaluation of cross-border transaction compliance

**Training and Communication**:
- Annual compliance training for all employees
- Specialized training for management and high-risk departments
- Compliance hotline and whistleblower protection system
- Regular compliance newsletters and bulletins

**Monitoring and Audit**:
- Internal compliance audits on a quarterly basis
- Transaction monitoring systems for financial red flags
- Due diligence procedures for new business partners
- Regular review of contracts for compliance clauses

### 4. How should companies handle internal investigations?

When a potential corporate crime is suspected:

1. **Preserve Evidence**: Do not destroy documents or data. Obstruction of justice (帮助毁灭、伪造证据罪, Art. 307) carries up to 3 years imprisonment.

2. **Engage Outside Counsel**: A China lawyer with corporate criminal defense experience should lead the investigation to maintain attorney-client privilege.

3. **Document Everything**: Create a detailed record of the investigation process, findings, and remedial actions.

4. **Consider Self-Reporting**: Under the voluntary disclosure provisions (自首, Art. 67), companies that self-report may receive reduced penalties.

5. **Implement Remedial Measures**: Document compliance improvements to demonstrate good faith.

### 5. What are the specific risks for technology companies in Shenzhen?

Shenzhen's tech sector faces particular criminal risks:

**Data-Related Crimes**:
- Unauthorized collection of personal data (侵犯公民个人信息罪, Art. 253-1)
- Illegal provision of data to third parties
- Failure to implement data security measures per the Data Security Law (《数据安全法》)

**Financial Crimes**:
- Crowdfunding platforms crossing into illegal deposit-taking
- Cryptocurrency-related business operations
- Cross-border payment processing without proper licensing

**Intellectual Property Crimes**:
- Patent infringement with commercial scale
- Software piracy and unauthorized distribution
- Trade secret misappropriation during employee transitions

### 6. What is the "compliance non-prosecution" system?

The Supreme People's Procuratorate (最高人民检察院) has been piloting a **compliance non-prosecution** (合规不起诉) system since 2020, now being formalized in 2025-2026. Under this system:

- Companies under criminal investigation can voluntarily establish compliance programs
- If the compliance program meets court-approved standards, prosecution may be suspended or dropped
- This represents a significant shift toward "compliance as defense" in Chinese corporate criminal law
- A Shenzhen lawyer experienced in this process can help companies qualify for non-prosecution

### 7. How should companies respond to dawn raids?

When public security authorities conduct a surprise investigation (搜查):

1. **Verify Identity**: Confirm the officers' identification and search warrant
2. **Contact Counsel Immediately**: Call your China lawyer before answering substantive questions
3. **Do Not Obstruct**: Passive non-cooperation differs from active obstruction
4. **Document Everything**: Record what is seized, who is questioned, and what statements are made
5. **Protect Privilege**: Identify and protect attorney-client privileged materials

### 8. What insurance products cover corporate criminal defense costs?

While China's insurance market for corporate criminal defense is still developing:

- Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance may cover defense costs
- Professional liability insurance for specific industries
- Some international insurers offer China-specific corporate crime coverage
- Budget ¥200,000-¥1,000,000 for typical corporate criminal defense
